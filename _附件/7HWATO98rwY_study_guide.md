# China’s Gilded Age: A Study Guide on Entrepreneurial Frenzy and Speculative Bubbles (2015–2018)

This study guide explores the period of extreme economic volatility and entrepreneurial fervor in China, primarily between 2015 and 2018. It examines the transition from idealistic tech startups in Beijing to the speculative madness of cryptocurrency in Shenzhen and the eventual emergence of Southeast Asian scam industries.

---

## I. Key Concepts and Historical Context

### 1. The 2015 Stock Market Bubble and "Man-Made" Bull Market
The year 2015 was defined by a massive surge in the Chinese stock market, peaking at 5,178 points. Unlike previous cycles, this was widely considered a "man-made" bull market, fueled by state-sanctioned narratives and high-leverage trading.
*   **The State Narrative:** On April 21, 2015, *People’s Daily* published an article claiming "4,000 points is the starting point of the bull market," framing the market's success as a vehicle for national strategic transformation.
*   **The Homes System:** This technology allowed for "shadow" margin trading, funneling bank wealth management funds into the stock market with leverage ratios as high as 1:5 or 1:10.
*   **The Crash and Intervention:** When regulators abruptly clamped down on leverage in June 2015, the market entered a "free fall." The government responded with aggressive measures, including banning "malicious short selling" and ordering state-owned funds and institutional investors to buy and hold regardless of losses.

### 2. The Beijing Startup Ecosystem: "Mass Entrepreneurship and Innovation"
Triggered by Premier Li Keqiang’s visit to 3W Coffee in Zhongguancun, the slogan "Mass Entrepreneurship and Innovation" ignited a national fever.
*   **The Two Faces of Zhongguancun:** 
    *   **3W Coffee:** Represented the mainstream, high-profile "Unicorn" culture focused on O2O (Online-to-Offline) and sharing economy models.
    *   **Garage Coffee:** Known as the "Yan'an of the Crypto Circle," it was a hub for early, marginalized believers in Bitcoin and blockchain.
*   **Idealism vs. Reality:** The period was marked by high "talent density," where graduates from elite universities (Tsinghua, Peking University) chose basement startups over stable corporate jobs.

### 3. The Shift in AI Ventures: CV vs. NLP
In 2016, Artificial Intelligence became the new venture capital (VC) darling. The industry split into two distinct paths:
*   **Computer Vision (CV):** Favored by VCs due to clear monetization paths, such as government surveillance (SkyNet) and autonomous driving.
*   **Natural Language Processing (NLP):** Faced a "lonely" path because it required longer development cycles and had less immediate "落地" (grounding/application) potential in the eyes of Chinese VCs, despite its long-term potential (as seen later in Large Language Models).

### 4. The 2017 ICO Craze and the "94" Regulatory Storm
The Initial Coin Offering (ICO) boom of 2017 saw technical idealism merge with speculative capital. 
*   **The "94" Event:** On September 4, 2017, the Chinese government banned ICOs and shut down local cryptocurrency exchanges. 
*   **Response to Regulation:** While some figures like Li Xiaolai initially complied, others like Justin Sun (Sun Yuchen) fled the country to continue operations, marking a shift toward "offshore" survival and more aggressive, less ethical business tactics.

### 5. Shenzhen’s "Family" and "Money" Culture
As the center of gravity shifted from Beijing to Shenzhen, the culture of the "Gilded Age" changed.
*   **The Shenzhen Model:** Characterized by "Wolf Culture" (aggression and speed) and "Family Culture" (multi-level marketing and insurance-style sales tactics).
*   **The "Plate" and "Communication" Circles:** These terms refer to the merging of cryptocurrency with Ponzi schemes (Plate/盘圈) and pyramid schemes (Communication/销圈).

## II. Short-Answer Practice Questions

**1. What was the significance of the "Homes System" in the 2015 stock market crash?**
The Homes System facilitated unregulated "outside-the-market" margin trading. It allowed investors to use extreme leverage (up to 1:10). When regulators ordered a sudden cleanup of these systems, it caused a liquidity panic and a "free fall" in stock prices.

**2. Describe the cultural difference between the Beijing and Shenzhen "Crypto Circles" as outlined in the text.**
Beijing’s circle was dominated by elite university graduates and technical "geeks" who maintained a degree of idealism and technical "face." Shenzhen’s circle was defined by "Wolf Culture," focused purely on profit, and utilized down-market, pyramid-scheme-style marketing tactics (the "Family" model) to attract unsophisticated investors.

**3. What is the "Catch and Kill" (一抓就死, 一放就亂) regulatory cycle described by the narrator?**
This describes a pattern where the government first allows an industry (like P2P or Crypto) to grow wildly without regulation ("One release leads to chaos"), followed by a sudden, brutal administrative crackdown that ignores existing rules and kills the industry overnight ("One catch leads to death").

**4. According to the text, why did the Southeast Asian "Scam Parks" become a destination for many Chinese youth after 2018?**
Many young people had accumulated massive debts through P2P lending and "Campus Loans" during the bubble years. Faced with "social death" and blacklisting in China, they fled to Southeast Asia to work in scam industries as a way to earn high wages and pay off their debts.

**5. How did Justin Sun (Sun Yuchen) differ from other entrepreneurs during the "94" regulatory storm?**
Unlike others who stayed to refund investors and comply with the ban, Sun fled to South Korea and then the U.S., refusing to refund the ICO immediately. He leveraged his "political speculation" background to maintain his project (TRON) at any cost, prioritizing survival and capital over regulatory compliance or "face."

## III. Essay Prompts for Deeper Exploration

1.  **The Price of Speculation:** Analyze how the "Gilded Age" of 2015–2018 fundamentally altered the career trajectories and psychological outlook of the "90s generation" in China. Contrast their early aspirations of "changing the world" with the eventual reality of debt and migration.
2.  **Technological Evolution vs. Capital Greed:** Using the examples of AI (NLP vs. CV) and Blockchain (Technical geeks vs. Ponzi schemes), discuss how the pressure for "predictable returns" from venture capital can stifle genuine technological innovation.
3.  **The Mirage of Wealth:** Evaluate the narrator's claim that many of the fortunes made during this period were merely "acting as a fund custodian for the state." Discuss this in the context of the P2P collapses and the eventual seizure of assets from figures like the "Huluwa" exchange owner.

## IV. Glossary of Important Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Gilded Age (镀金时代)** | A term used to describe the period between 2015–2018 in China, characterized by extreme economic bubbles, a frenzy of startup activity, and widespread speculation. |
| **Mass Entrepreneurship and Innovation** | A government-led slogan and policy initiative designed to encourage citizens to start businesses and innovate, sparked by high-profile visits to startup hubs. |
| **94 Regulatory Storm (94 监管风暴)** | The September 4, 2017, directive by Chinese regulators that banned ICOs and effectively shuttered domestic crypto exchanges. |
| **ICO (Initial Coin Offering)** | A fundraising mechanism where new projects sell their own underlying crypto tokens in exchange for Bitcoin or Ethereum. |
| **P2P Lending** | Peer-to-peer internet lending platforms that grew rapidly in China before a series of massive "thunderstorms" (defaults and collapses) led to their total ban. |
| **Plate/Communication Circles (盘圈/销圈)** | Slang terms for the speculative cryptocurrency sectors in Shenzhen that focused on Ponzi schemes (plates) and pyramid/MLM schemes (communication). |
| **Social Death (社会性死亡)** | A state where an individual is blacklisted from the financial and legal systems (unable to take high-speed rail, use credit cards, etc.) due to unpaid debts or legal issues. |
| **Homes System** | A technical system used in 2015 to bypass margin trading regulations, allowing massive amounts of leveraged capital to enter the stock market. |
| **NLP (Natural Language Processing)** | A branch of AI focused on the interaction between computers and human language; cited as a "lonely" investment path in 2016 compared to Computer Vision. |
| **O2O (Online-to-Offline)** | A business model designed to bring online customers to physical businesses; a major focus of the 2015 startup craze (e.g., bike-sharing, food delivery). |
