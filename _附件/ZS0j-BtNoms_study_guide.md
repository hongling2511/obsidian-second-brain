# Study Guide: Top AI Tools for Finance Professionals

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the emerging landscape of Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools designed for finance professionals as of 2025/2026. It categorizes tools by their specific functions—ranging from automated reporting and financial planning to audit and expense management—and provides practice materials to reinforce the concepts.

---

## Overview of AI in Finance
As the landscape of financial technology evolves, incorporating AI tools has become essential for finance professionals aiming to increase efficiency and accuracy. These tools are designed to save hours of manual labor by automating repetitive tasks, enhancing data analysis, and providing institutional-level insights.

---

## Key AI Tools and Functionalities

### 1. Reporting and Analysis Tools
*   **Zebra AI:** This tool is specifically designed for automated reporting. Users can upload Excel files, and the tool generates management-ready reports. A key feature is its ability to provide automated commentaries, which are written descriptions explaining the data presented in graphs. It is the sister tool to Zebra BI and follows International Business Communication Standards (IBCS).
*   **Bigdata.com:** Focused on investment and stock analysis, this tool functions similarly to a chatbot but is powered by 20 years of institutional-quality data from Rabbitback. Users can create watchlists, generate graphs, and receive live insights through a chat interface.

### 2. Accounting and Expense Management
*   **Mouse:** An expense management system that utilizes AI to recognize data on invoices and receipts. It automatically books these entries into the system and can handle accounting allocation, categorizing employee spend into the correct accounts.
*   **Forast:** This tool automates the finance close process and heavy accounting operations. It features AI-driven reconciliations and variance analysis. It also allows users to describe repetitive journal entries in natural language, which the machine then "codes" into the system without manual figure entry.
*   **Ryu:** Focuses on the "room of receipts" problem. It scans receipts, books them automatically, and checks for compliance with company policies. It is particularly adept at handling complex Value Added Tax (VAT) rules and exceptions, saving time for both employees and the finance department.

### 3. Financial Planning and Analysis (FP&A)
*   **Runway:** Designed to make financial planning accessible and engaging, Runway uses an intuitive interface compared to a video game or "Notion for finance." It allows teams to build financial models, query them using AI, generate narratives for management, and perform multi-scenario planning. It is marketed toward startups and growing companies.

### 4. Specialized Financial Operations
*   **Trillion:** A versatile tool covering three major finance areas:
    *   **Audit:** Helps both auditors and audited companies by cross-referencing financial statements to identify discrepancies.
    *   **Lease Accounting:** Reads lease agreements and proposes bookings and financial statement notes.
    *   **Revenue Recognition:** Automates the step-by-step revenue recognition process for US GAAP and IFRS, utilized by major firms like Siemens and Walmart.
*   **Puzzle:** An accounting automation tool for startups. It requires high-quality subledger data through APIs (such as Stripe for revenue or Gusto for payroll). When fed clean data, it can automate up to 99% of the accounting process and integrates with communication tools like Slack.
*   **Zapliance:** A European tool (Germany) specifically designed for SAP users. It identifies duplicate invoices and discovers recoverable VAT that may have been missed due to incorrect coding or system errors.
*   **Numra:** An Irish tool featuring a virtual assistant named "Mari." It sits on top of existing systems to manage accounts receivable and accounts payable, and can automate complex Excel tasks that are traditionally difficult for humans to manage.

## Essay Prompts for Deeper Exploration

1.  **The Impact of AI on the Audit Process:** Using Trillion as a case study, analyze how AI-driven cross-referencing and automated statement analysis might change the relationship between auditors and the companies they audit. Consider the implications for accuracy and the reduction of manual labor.
2.  **The Shift in Startup Accounting:** Examine the requirements of tools like Puzzle and Runway. Discuss how the need for "clean data" and "strong APIs" is shifting the responsibility of finance professionals from data entry to data architecture and system integration.
3.  **Human-AI Collaboration in Manufacturing Finance:** Using Numra’s "Mari" assistant as a reference, discuss the potential for virtual assistants to manage complex legacy tasks like accounts receivable/payable and intricate Excel work in manufacturing sectors.
4.  **Regulatory Compliance and AI:** Analyze how tools like Ryu and Zapliance handle complex tax regulations (such as VAT). Discuss why these tasks are considered "not fun for humans" and how AI ensures higher compliance with company and governmental policies.

## Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Accounting Allocation** | The process of assigning employee spending or expenses to the correct categories or departments within a financial system. |
| **API (Application Programming Interface)** | A set of rules that allows different software entities to communicate. In the context of Puzzle, strong APIs are required to pull clean data from subledgers. |
| **Commentaries** | Automated written descriptions generated by AI to explain the data and trends found in financial graphs. |
| **Finance Close** | The process of finalizing a company's financial records at the end of a specific period (monthly, quarterly, or annually). |
| **IBCS Standards** | International Business Communication Standards; a set of rules for the visual design of charts and reports for clear communication. |
| **IFRS / US GAAP** | The two primary sets of accounting standards (International Financial Reporting Standards and Generally Accepted Accounting Principles) used globally for financial reporting. |
| **Journal Entries** | The record of financial transactions in the accounting books of a business. |
| **Revenue Recognition** | An accounting principle that outlines the specific conditions under which revenue is recognized and recorded. |
| **Subledger** | A detailed record of transactions (e.g., payroll, revenue, or expenses) that feeds into the general ledger. |
| **Variance Analysis** | The quantitative investigation of the difference between actual and planned behavior in finance. |
| **VAT (Value Added Tax)** | A type of consumption tax that is placed on a product whenever value is added at a stage of production and at final sale. |
